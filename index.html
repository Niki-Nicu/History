Chapter Timeline — Ten-Chapter Flow with Civilisational Transitions
1
What, Where, How and When?
2 MYA – 600 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • History = past of ALL living beings; dates from written records (inscriptions, manuscripts)
  • Historians use BC/AD or BCE/CE; BCE = Before Common Era
  • Sources: archaeological (material), literary, oral
  • Narmada Valley — earliest human habitation in India (~2 MYA)
  • River valleys (Indus, Ganga) = centres of early civilisations
  • Deccan Plateau — black soil (Regur); tribal habitations
▸ Key Concepts
BCE/CE Archaeology Manuscripts Inscriptions Coins Oral tradition
▸ Sites / Sources
  • Narmada Valley (earliest habitation)
  • ASI — Archaeological Survey of India
  • Subtraction method: 2000 BCE = 2000+2024 = ~4000 years ago
UPSC Sig · Foundation chapter — defines the tools historians use. Prelims often asks BCE vs BC; sources classification (material vs literary vs oral); and river valley significance.
Memory · "What WHERE how WHEN" = 4Ws of History. Narmada = oldest; Inscriptions = most reliable primary source.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 1 → Ch 2
Once we know how to read the past, we turn to the earliest chapter of human life: hunter-gatherers slowly learning to domesticate plants and animals. The shift from foraging to farming — the Neolithic Revolution — is the single greatest change in human prehistory, creating the conditions for permanent settlement, surplus, and ultimately civilisation.
Tools of History → Earliest Human Lives
2
From Hunting-Gathering to Growing Food
2 MYA – 3000 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Palaeolithic (Old Stone Age): nomadic, microlithic tools, cave paintings (Bhimbetka)
  • Mesolithic (Middle Stone Age): transitional, smaller tools, semi-nomadic
  • Neolithic (New Stone Age): agriculture, domestication, polished stone tools
  • Mehrgarh (Balochistan): earliest farming site in subcontinent (~7000 BCE); barley, wheat; cattle
  • Inamgaon (Maharashtra): Chalcolithic village; burial under house floor
  • Chirand (Bihar), Burzahom (Kashmir): other Neolithic sites
▸ Key Concepts
Palaeolithic Neolithic Domestication Mehrgarh Bhimbetka Microlithic
▸ Important Sites
  • Bhimbetka: Madhya Pradesh; rock paintings; Palaeolithic–Mesolithic
  • Mehrgarh: earliest agri; wheat/barley; c.7000 BCE
  • Inamgaon: Chalcolithic; Maharashtra; burials
UPSC Sig · Prelims: Mehrgarh dates, Bhimbetka (UNESCO WHS 2003), difference between Palaeolithic and Neolithic, Chalcolithic = Copper-Stone Age. New NCERT merges old Ch2+Ch3 here.
Memory · OLD-STONE = Bhimbetka caves. NEW-STONE = Mehrgarh farms. Chalco = copper knife + stone sickle. Inamgaon = buried at home.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 2 → Ch 3
Agriculture created food surplus. Surplus enabled populations to grow, specialists to emerge (potters, weavers, traders), and settlements to expand. By ~2600 BCE this process culminated in the Harappan/Indus Valley Civilisation — the world's largest Bronze Age urban culture, with planned cities, drainage, and standardised weights.
Farming Villages → First Cities
3
In the Earliest Cities
2600 – 1900 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • IVC (Harappan Civ.) = ~2600–1900 BCE; over 1400 sites; 5 major cities
  • Script: undeciphered; written right-to-left; ~400 signs; seals
  • Town planning: grid streets, burnt-brick, covered drainage, granaries
  • Trade: Mesopotamia (Meluhha = Harappa); copper, carnelian beads, cotton
  • No temples, weapons of war found; Priest-King figure; Mother Goddess terracotta
  • Decline theories: climate change, Ghaggar-Hakra river drying, flooding, invasion (outdated)
▸ Key Sites
Harappa Mohenjo-daro Dholavira Lothal Kalibangan Rakhigarhi
▸ Unique Features
  • Great Bath: Mohenjo-daro; ritual bathing
  • Dockyard: Lothal (Gujarat)
  • Signboard: Dholavira (largest inscription)
  • Dancing Girl: bronze; Mohenjo-daro
UPSC Sig · Prelims heavy: undeciphered script, no iron/horse/coins, Lothal dockyard, Dholavira signboard, Rakhigarhi as largest site. Mesopotamia trade = Meluhha reference in cuneiform texts.
Memory · IVC = 3Ps: Planned cities, Pictographic script (undeciphered), Peaceful society (no weapons). Lothal = port, Dholavira = signboard, Rakhigarhi = largest.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 3 → Ch 4
After c.1900 BCE the Harappan cities declined — populations dispersed, some migrating eastward to the Ganga-Yamuna Doab. Simultaneously, Vedic pastoral communities composed the Rigveda. This transition is recorded not in cities but in texts and burials — the Vedas, Painted Grey Ware, and megalithic graves now become our primary windows into this new world.
Urban Harappa → Vedic Texts & Megalithic Graves
4
What Books and Burials Tell Us
1500 – 600 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Rigveda: oldest (c.1500 BCE); 1028 hymns; oral transmission; Sanskrit
  • 4 Vedas: Rig (hymns), Sama (music), Yajur (rituals), Atharva (spells)
  • Vedic society: tribal (jana); cattle wealth (godhana); rajanyas/kshatriyas ruling
  • Megaliths: large stone burial monuments; south India (Brahmagiri) & NE; Iron Age
  • Brahmagiri (Karnataka): megaliths + iron tools + cattle bones
  • Painted Grey Ware (PGW): associated with later Vedic period; Ganga-Yamuna Doab
▸ Key Concepts
Rigveda Megalith Godhana Jana PGW Brahmagiri
▸ Burial Practices
  • Megaliths = stone-lined pits; iron tools, pottery with dead
  • Inamgaon: burial under house floors (Chalcolithic)
  • Brahmagiri: elaborate megalithic; S. India; ~800 BCE
  • Objects buried = wealth indicators
UPSC Sig · Prelims: Rigveda composition date, 4 Vedas names, Megalith definition, Brahmagiri site, Painted Grey Ware (PGW) vs Northern Black Polished Ware (NBPW) — important trap.
Memory · SAME YA RIG AT = Sama, Yajur, Rig, Atharva. Megalith = MEGA + LITH (big stone). Brahmagiri = south India iron + megaliths.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 4 → Ch 5
The Vedic pastoral society gradually transitioned from tribal clan (jana) to territorial unit (janapada), and later to larger mahajanapadas as iron technology enabled forest-clearance, wet rice cultivation, and surplus taxation. By 600 BCE, 16 powerful states (shodasha mahajanapadas) had emerged — marking India's first age of territorial kingdoms and republics.
Vedic Tribes (Jana) → Janapadas & Kingdoms
5
Kingdoms, Kings and an Early Republic
600 – 300 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • 16 Mahajanapadas (Anguttara Nikaya list): Vajji, Magadha, Kosala, Kuru, Panchala, Avanti, Gandhara…
  • Vajji: Gana-sangha (republic/oligarchy); capital Vaishali; Buddha's contemporary
  • Magadha: strongest; iron ore + Ganga trade routes; Bimbisara (first expansionist king)
  • Rajasuya & Ashvamedha yajnas = legitimise king's power
  • Northern Black Polished Ware (NBPW): luxury pottery; mahajanapada period
  • Punch-marked coins: earliest coins in India; silver; tax/trade
▸ Key Concepts
Mahajanapada Gana-sangha Vajji NBPW Punch coins Rajasuya
▸ Personalities
  • Bimbisara: Magadha; diplomatic marriages; powerful king
  • Ajatashatru: conquered Vajji; catapult weapon
  • Chandragupta Maurya: later overthrew Nanda dynasty
UPSC Sig · Prelims: 16 mahajanapadas list (Anguttara Nikaya = Buddhist source), Vajji = world's earliest republic, NBPW pottery, punch-marked coins, Magadha's geographical advantages.
Memory · VAJJI = world's First Republic. Magadha = 3 advantages: Iron + Elephants + Ganga. NBPW = shiny luxury of mahajanapada era.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 5 → Ch 6
As kingdoms consolidated power, social inequalities deepened — rigid varna system, elaborate Brahmanical rituals, expensive sacrifices. This created widespread social and spiritual questioning. Wandering thinkers (shramanas) challenged Vedic authority. The 6th century BCE saw a remarkable burst of new philosophical schools — Buddhism, Jainism, the Upanishads — all questioning the old order.
Kingdoms & Social Hierarchy → Spiritual & Philosophical Revolt
6
New Questions and Ideas
600 – 200 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Upanishads: philosophical texts; part of Vedas; discuss atman-brahman, moksha
  • Mahavira (599–527 BCE): 24th Tirthankara; Jainism; non-violence (ahimsa), truth (satya), non-stealing, celibacy, non-possession (5 vows)
  • Buddha (563–483 BCE): Siddharth Gautama; Kshatriya; Enlightenment at Bodh Gaya; first sermon at Sarnath (Dhamma-cakka-ppavattana)
  • Four Noble Truths + Eightfold Path; Middle Way; Nirvana
  • Buddhist Sangha: community of monks/nuns; democratic; Patimokkha rules
  • Jataka tales: previous lives of Buddha; popular literature
▸ Key Concepts
Ahimsa Nirvana Sangha Upanishads Tirthankara Shramana
▸ Key Diff: Buddhism vs Jainism
  • Both reject Vedic authority; both accept karma/rebirth
  • Jainism: extreme ahimsa (even microbes); Digambara/Shvetambara
  • Buddhism: Middle Way; does not require asceticism; Theravada/Mahayana
  • Sarnath = Buddha's 1st sermon; Bodh Gaya = enlightenment
UPSC Sig · One of the most heavily tested chapters. Buddha's dates, 4 Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, Mahavira's 5 vows, Sarnath vs Bodh Gaya vs Kushinagar, Buddhist Sangha as democratic institution.
Memory · Buddha's 4Ts: Truth of suffering, Thirst (cause), Termination, Therapy (path). Mahavira's 5 vows = ASTAC (Ahimsa, Satya, Asteya, Celibacy, Aparigraha).
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 6 → Ch 7
Buddhism spread rapidly because it had royal patronage. Chandragupta Maurya built the first pan-Indian empire; his grandson Ashoka converted to Buddhism after the horrific Kalinga war (261 BCE) and used his empire to spread dhamma — an ideology of non-violence, tolerance, and welfare — becoming history's most remarkable example of imperial self-transformation.
New Ideas + Magadha Power → Mauryan Empire & Ashoka
7
Ashoka, the Emperor Who Gave Up War
321 – 185 BCE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Mauryan Empire: Chandragupta (321 BCE) → Bindusara → Ashoka (268–232 BCE)
  • Kalinga War (261 BCE): ~1.5 lakh killed; Ashoka's transformation; Rock Edict XIII
  • Ashoka's Dhamma: non-violence, respect for elders, religious tolerance, welfare (wells, hospitals)
  • Inscriptions: Brahmi (most), Kharosthi (NW), Greek/Aramaic; James Prinsep deciphered Brahmi (1837)
  • Arthashastra: Kautilya/Chanakya; statecraft manual; espionage, taxation, administration
  • Megasthenes: Greek ambassador; wrote Indica; described Mauryan court
▸ Key Concepts
Dhamma Kalinga Brahmi Arthashastra Megasthenes Rock Edict
▸ Key Inscriptions
  • Rock Edict XIII: Kalinga war remorse
  • Minor Rock Edicts: Ashoka's personal conversion
  • Pillar Edicts: later reign; law of Dhamma
  • Taxila: NW satrapy; Hellenistic trade hub
UPSC Sig · Most tested chapter of NCERT 6. Prelims: Prinsep deciphered Brahmi, Megasthenes wrote Indica, Kalinga = Odisha, Rock Edict XIII = Kalinga war, Arthashastra author.
Memory · Ashoka = KALINGA → K-shattered him → converted. PRINSEP = deciphered Brahmi 1837. Dhamma = 5 Ws: Welfare, non-Violence, elders, tolerance, religious Wisdom.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 7 → Ch 8
After Ashoka (d.232 BCE), the Mauryan Empire fragmented. But its infrastructure — roads, towns, trade networks — survived. The post-Mauryan centuries (200 BCE – 300 CE) saw remarkable economic growth: thriving towns, guild systems (shrenis), long-distance trade, Roman coin imports, and the rise of Silk Route commerce. Ordinary village and urban life becomes the new historical subject.
Mauryan Infrastructure → Thriving Villages & Towns
8
Vital Villages, Thriving Towns
200 BCE – 300 CE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Three categories of farmers: large landowners (gahapati), independent farmers, agricultural labourers (dasa-karmakara)
  • Iron ploughshare + irrigation tanks (Sringaverapura) = agricultural revolution
  • Shreni (guilds): merchants, craftsmen; issued own coins; regulated trade
  • Mathura: major city; sculpture, cotton; crossroads of trade routes
  • Arikamedu (Pondicherry): Indo-Roman trade; amphorae, Arretine ware, beads
  • Sangam literature (Tamil): earliest Tamil texts; describes ports, paddy, silk, pepper
▸ Key Concepts
Shreni Gahapati Arikamedu Sangam Silk Route NBPW
▸ Sites & Sources
  • Arikamedu: Roman amphorae, glass; S. India port
  • Mathura: Kushan sculpture; Buddhist art
  • Nagarjunakonda: Buddhist site; Andhra
  • Sangam poems: Purananuru, Akananuru; Tamil heroic poetry
UPSC Sig · Prelims: Arikamedu = Indo-Roman trade (Arretine pottery), Shreni = guilds (medieval form = sreni), gahapati = landed gentry, Sangam = Tamil literature earliest, Sringaverapura irrigation.
Memory · ARIKAMEDU = ARi = ARRETine (Roman pottery). Shreni = guild = think UNION of craftsmen. Gahapati = GReat householder = landed farmer.
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 8 → Ch 9
The economic prosperity of the post-Mauryan era provided the resource base for new empires. The Guptas (320–550 CE) unified much of northern India; the Pallavas and Chalukyas dominated the south; Harsha briefly reunified the north (606–647 CE). These were not just military empires but cultural patronage states — funding temples, literature, mathematics, and astronomy. The age of classical India had arrived.
Trade-Wealth & Guilds → Gupta & Regional Empires
9
New Empires and Kingdoms
320 – 750 CE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Guptas (320–550 CE): Chandragupta I, Samudragupta (Allahabad Pillar), Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya)
  • Samudragupta: Allahabad Pillar inscription (Harishena composed); 4 policy types: annexation, tribute, gifts, alliance
  • Fa-Hien (399–414 CE): Chinese pilgrim; visited Gupta India; describes peaceful prosperous society
  • Prashastis: eulogies composed for kings; inscriptions; poetic accounts
  • Harsha (606–647 CE): Kanauj; patron of Buddhism; Xuanzang (Hiuen Tsang) visited
  • Pallavas (Kanchipuram), Chalukyas (Badami): S. India; Pulakesi II defeated Harsha
▸ Key Concepts
Prashasti Samudragupta Fa-Hien Xuanzang Harsha Nalanda
▸ Key Personalities
  • Samudragupta: "Napoleon of India" (V.A. Smith); Veena-player; poet
  • Chandragupta II: Vikramaditya; Kalidasa's patron; Iron Pillar Delhi
  • Harsha: wrote plays (Ratnavali, Priyadarshika); Harshacharita by Banabhatta
  • Pulakesi II: Chalukya; defeated Harsha at Narmada (634 CE)
UPSC Sig · Prelims: Samudragupta = Allahabad Pillar + Harishena, Fa-Hien vs Xuanzang (Gupta vs Harsha), Prashasti definition, Pulakesi II defeat of Harsha, Nalanda University founded by Kumaragupta I.
Memory · Gupta timeline: C1→Sam→C2(Vikra)→Skanda→Kumara→Buda. Fa-Hien = Gupta era. Xuanzang = Harsha era. Pulakesi = PULLED Harsha back (defeated him).
→
⟶ Civilisational Transition · Ch 9 → Ch 10
Political power and imperial patronage now found their most lasting expression not in conquest but in cultural creation. The same wealth that funded Gupta armies also funded the Ajanta murals, Sanskrit literature (Kalidasa), iron-pillar metallurgy, mathematical innovations (Aryabhata), and the great cave temples — India's classical age produced monuments that have endured for 1500 years.
Gupta & Post-Gupta Empires → Classical Art, Literature & Science
10
Buildings, Paintings and Books
200 BCE – 700 CE
▸ UPSC Prelims Facts
  • Stupas: Buddhist monuments; relics of Buddha inside; Sanchi (MP) = finest; Amaravati (AP)
  • Stupa structure: anda (dome), harmika, yashti (pillar), toranas (gateways), pradakshina path
  • Ajanta Caves (Maharashtra): Buddhist; paintings (tempera); Gupta period; UNESCO WHS
  • Ellora: Hindu + Buddhist + Jain caves; rock-cut; Kailash Temple (Rashtrakuta)
  • Kalidasa: Shakuntala, Meghaduta, Raghuvansha; Gupta court; greatest Sanskrit poet
  • Aryabhata: mathematician/astronomer; calculated pi, earth's rotation, solar year; Aryabhatiya
▸ Key Concepts
Stupa Ajanta Kalidasa Aryabhata Nalanda Toranas
▸ Key Sites & Works
  • Sanchi Stupa: Ashoka built; gateways added later; Madhya Pradesh
  • Ajanta: 29 caves; paintings of Jataka tales; 2nd BCE – 7th CE
  • Nalanda: Buddhist university; Bihar; ~5th CE; Xuanzang studied here
  • Iron Pillar: Delhi; Gupta; rust-free; metallurgical marvel
UPSC Sig · Prelims: Stupa parts (anda/harmika/yashti/toranas), Sanchi vs Amaravati stupas, Ajanta = paintings (NOT sculptures), Aryabhata = Aryabhatiya + zero concept, Kalidasa's works list.
Memory · Stupa = AYHT: Anda + Yashti + Harmika + Torana. Ajanta = PAINT. Ellora = CUT (rock-cut). Kalidasa = 3S: Shakuntala, Shishupalvadha... wait — Meghaduta + Shakuntala + Raghuvamsha.

Classification of Historical Sources
Manuscripts Inscriptions Coins Seals Megaliths Monuments Oral Tradition Pottery Prashastis
Inscriptions (most reliable; fixed; cannot be altered) vs Manuscripts (copied by scribes; can have errors). Coins: rulers, trade routes, dates. Seals: IVC identity, trade. Megaliths: burial practices. Prashastis: royal praise poems (biased but informative). Sangam poems: social life of Tamil people. Jataka tales: Buddhist previous lives; social history.

Common Conceptual Distinctions — UPSC
Manuscript
VS
Inscription
Manuscripts = handwritten; can be copied/altered. Inscriptions = on stone/metal; cannot be altered = MORE RELIABLE primary source.
Harappan Society
VS
Vedic Society
IVC = urban, undeciphered script, no iron, no horse, peaceful. Vedic = pastoral, Sanskrit oral, iron (later), horse (Ashvamedha), warfare.
Janapada
VS
Mahajanapada
Janapada = "foot of the tribe"; smaller early territory. Mahajanapada = "great territory"; 16 powerful states from ~600 BCE.
Buddhism
VS
Jainism
Buddhism = Middle Way; rejects extreme asceticism; Nirvana. Jainism = extreme ahimsa; complete non-violence (even microbes); Moksha through tapas.
Stupa
VS
Temple
Stupa = Buddhist; dome-shaped; houses relics; no idol inside. Temple = Hindu; spire (shikhara); idol in garbhagriha. Both = religious architecture.
Megalith
VS
Cairn burial
Megalith = "big stone" burial structure; Iron Age; S. India & NE; Brahmagiri. Cairn = stone-heap over grave. Both = burial markers but different scale.
Fa-Hien
VS
Xuanzang
Fa-Hien = Gupta era (Chandragupta II, 399–414 CE). Xuanzang = Harsha era (629–645 CE). Both = Chinese pilgrims but different centuries!
PGW
VS
NBPW
PGW (Painted Grey Ware) = later Vedic / early mahajanapada; grey with black designs. NBPW (N. Black Polished Ware) = mahajanapada peak; lustrous black luxury ware.
